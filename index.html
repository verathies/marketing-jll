CPC

CPC stands for cost per click advertising. Here the advertiser pays when a click is made on an ad.

CPL

Here the advertiser pays when a lead form is completed and submitted. CPL is common where it is unlikely that someone will buy immediately. It can be a very effective way to buy. The CPL differs on how much information you are requesting from the lead.

CPS/ CPA

You only pay if a purchase is made. This is relatively low-risk way to buy media because the advertiser only pays when revenue is driven. Usually, media isn’t sold this way because of the risk in the ad purchase.

CPD

In mobile app marketing, CPD refers to media programs where the advertiser pays for every installed app. Lots of app marketing is purchased CPD, because it is a fast way to drive installs.

CPM

CPM is one of the most common ways of buying digital media. You pay for every time your ad loads on a page or in an app. It’s a simple way to buy.
